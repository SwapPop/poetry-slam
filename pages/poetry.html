Gravity

The diamond on my finger matches the stars
as I swoon in your arms like a wave
broken on the shore. I’ve danced to your tune
long enough that I know every move—
in and out, up and down, over and through:
I bend to you like the tide to the moon.

But the earth pulls on us, heavy,
it calls you to fall to me forever but never
to touch the waters that mirror your lune.
You say turn on three: “One, two…”
I step too soon and wonder: Does the tide
ever tire of pleasing the moon?

Glossed

My eyes double hers in the halogen
light of Sephora’s checkout line.
Her eyes are vacant, mine expectant,
our smoky stares cemented in the
silky polish of a magazine page.
Her cameraman must have used an
ethereal lens. A soft box distributes
light evenly across her pixie features
and reflectors bounce glowing orbs
into the pupils of her eyes.
Her software-softened skin has the
sheen of melted plastic and her neck
rises above her shoulders like
stretched taffy. Leather bands
wrap her arms like a straight jacket.
My face is engorged in front of hers,
a ghostly reflection—distorted
by the curving bend of the page.
I look up at the lady across from me
at the counter. She dressed like Miss America
for work, her eyes lined in blue and purple.
I hand her my money and leave
the store. At home I leave orange
makeup wipes on the bathroom floor.

Why I Write Poetry:

Response to Major Jackson

Because I can’t reach the top shelf of my kitchen cabinet
Because the sun is a globe of fire
Because sometimes I wear headphones and look out the window
to avoid other people
Because there are bookstores on London corners that I’ve not yet seen
Because once my mom threw up off the side of a boat and came face to face with a
whale
Because I’m not sure I want my children to believe in fairytales
Because when it rains the brush by my house turns purple and it reminds me that
the world bruises too
Because he said once that he saw me everywhere he looked
Because the sky is dappled with stars that have already burned out
Because I grew up singing that I was a child of God
Because the songbird’s bones were designed for flight
Because I used to fall asleep to the humming noise of my parents’ television set
Because another girl was shot dead on a college campus yesterday and there’s no
reason for any of it
Because the last time I called my Grandpa Stan he still remembered my name
Because the rain in Spain stays mainly in the plain
Because humans measure time by the length of shadows
Because in second grade I watched from behind a red brick pillar as the other girls
played Little Sally Walker
Because I spent this afternoon studying weighted blankets instead of business
strategies
Because its been proven that if you never use your eyes you’ll eventually lose your
sight

Mother of Eve:

You’re like a
specter
snuggled just behind
my shoulder,
or a memory
I can’t quite place:
the smell
of hand soap
Momma used
when I was younger,
or old perfume
in Grandma’s embrace.
There’s something
so tangible
about you,
I can almost feel you there,
but when I sketch in
the shadows of your frame
my own silhouette
begins to appear.

Inspiration:

It comes
like bittersweet chocolate
to a starving tongue.
Unexpected
not unwelcome.
I salivate,
storm, and
sweat:
afraid that just a touch
is not enough.
I grasp at half of the moon,
an unsung verse,
and abandoned love--
take them under my flapping wings,
and beg them to survive
and thrive
within me.
It’s okay.
I say.
Don’t be alarmed.
These things will come and go,
ebb and flow,
I won’t fight their retreat.
Instead I’ll make friends
with every imperfect end.
