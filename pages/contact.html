To love and spring:

To what does love belong if not to spring?
With tiger-tails and jasmine pales alight
upon the wind, their sweetened fruits to bring
to starving tongues’ all but undone delight.

Soft petals cloud the air like dusted flecks--
in sun they’re seen but masked by falling dusk.
And blossoms blooming wide with frosted neck,
soon cast aside, all dried and dyed and husked.

So too is love a thing that n’er outlasts
the night. A sunny face peeks ‘round the clouds
in time to warm soft hands and faces fast,
but hides again before the shadows, cowed.

So spring and love hold hands in certain things:
bring all the beauty, leave, and make it sting.
